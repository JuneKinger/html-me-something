Exploring Goa, India - my roots!

About Goa:

Goa is the smallest state in India and offers a fascinating mix of Indian and Portuguese cultures. It is situated on the southwestern coast of India. It is a former Portuguese province. Goa is visited by large numbers of international and domestic tourists each year and is famous for its white sand beaches, nightlife, places of worship, and rich architecture. Growing up, my family would spend Summers here in our ancestral home, although I was raised in Mumbai which is 365 miles from Goa.

North Goa:

North Goa is one of the two districts that make up the state of Goa in India. North Goa beaches are more crowded than South Goa beaches because of its fun, lively atmosphere year round.

South Goa:

South Goa is known for quieter beaches, luxury resorts, and less crowded areas. Palolem beach is where the movie "The Bourne Supremacy" was filmed!

Anjuna, North Goa

Anjuna has seen the hippie culture way back in the past and whose flea market held every Wednesday is still one of the largest I have ever seen! This beach is a favorite among foreigners. The stretch of Anjuna Beach is filled with water activities, cafes, coffee shops, family run-guesthouses, and beach bars. Anjuna is also a place known for its small rocks by the sea side. People staying in nearby hotels often walk up to the sea and sit here to watch the sunrise and sip tea or coffe. One of the most carried out activities include jet skiing and para gliding.

Dudhsagar, North Goa

Dudhsagar Falls (means Sea of Milk), during the monsoon season, is fed by rains and form a huge force of water.

The Cathedral of Jesus, Old Goa, North Goa

This stunning cathedral is a part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site. The church is about 400 years old and located in Old Goa, which was once the capital of Goa during the days of the Portuguese rule. The church was built around 1605.

Fort Aguada

Fort Aguada and its lighthouse is a well-preserved seventeenth-century Portuguese fort standing on Sinquerim Beach, overlooking the Arabian Sea. The fort was constructed in 1612 to guard against the Dutch and the Marathas. A freshwater spring within the fort provided water supply to the ships that used to stop by.

Flea Market

Some great bargains to be had and also some fun haggling with the market traders. This unique market sets up every Wednesday and sells a wide variety of stuff, ranging from spices, clothes, jewellery, collectibles, etc. My cousin mans a stall here! It is one of the largest flea markets I have ever seen!

Mirmar Beach

Miramar beach is situated in the Goan capital of Panjim, also known as Panaji and is one of the most visited beaches of Goa. Situated at the confluence of a river and the Arabian Sea

Palolem beach

Palolem Beach is situated in Canacona in southern Goa, India. The beach attracts many international tourists, mainly during the winter season between November and March. It is considered to be one of the region's most beautiful beaches.

Se cathedral

The Se Cathedral was built to commemorate the victory of the Portuguese under Afonso de Albuquerque over a Muslim army, leading to the capture of the city of Goa in 1510. Since the day of the victory happened to be on the feast of Saint Catherine, the cathedral was dedicated to her. It had two towers but one collapsed in 1776 and was never rebuilt.

Colva beach

Colva beach has pristine white sand and aqua water. Sports enthusiasts can try out snorkeling, speed-boat riding, parasailing, jet skiing, banana ride, motorboat rides, and paragliding for a wonderful beach sports experience.

Oldest Portuguese mansion

The oldest Portuguese mansion, Braganza house, Chandor is an astonishing mansion that dates back to the 16th century and occupies one side of the village square in Chandor. The elaborate mansion, which is spread over about 10,000 square meters, has been divided into two disparate wings (eastern and western wings). Two generations of the Braganza family live here!
